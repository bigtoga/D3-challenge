Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

This is an interactive tool - as you click any of the x or y axis links, the chart will update so that you can compare those two variables.

Comparing "People in poverty" to "People who lack healthcare coverage"

It's no surprise that individuals with lower incomes (such as those below the poverty line) also have a lower statistical probability of having healthcare coverage. With this graph, you can clearly see that Texas is a "Lone Star State" in that it is the only state that has both a "high" percentage of its population in poverty (17.2%) but also has the highest percentage of un-insured citizens in the country (24.9% - nearly 5 full percentage points above the 2nd place Mississippi).
